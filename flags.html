Sexualities, and what they mean

Gay

Gay is when you're homosexual (having a sexual attraction towards people of one's own sex.) which especially is used towards a man.

The Colours

Pink: Sex
Red: Life
Orange: Healing
Yellow: Sunlight
Green: Nature
Turquoise: Magic/ Art
Indigo: Serenity
Violet: Spirit

Lesbian

Lesbian is typically a homosexual woman. (homosexual defined at gay)

The Colours

There have been multiple versions of the lesbian pride flag. But all the different shades of pink, orange, white and red were used to represent different types of feminity.

Asexual

Asexual is the lack of sexual attraction towards others.

The Colours

Black: Asexuality
Grey: Gray-asexuality and demisexuality
White: Non-asexual partners and allies
Purple: The Community.

Bisexual

Bisexual is when a person is attracted to more than one sex or gender identity.

The Colours

pink: attraction to the same gender identity
blue: Attraction to those that identify as a different gender.
Purple: Attraction to two genders.

Pansexual

Pansexual is when a person is not limited to a sexual desire or attraction regardless of their gender identity or sexual orientation in summary, they can be attracted to anyone.

The Colours

Pink: Attraction towards female identities
Blue: Attraction towards male identities
Yellow: Attraction towards genderqueer, non-binary, agender, androgynous etc.

Heterosexual

Heterosexual is when a person's only attraction is toward people of the opposite sex. Most name the term "heterosexual" as straight.

The Flag

The Straight flag uses black and white colours, and it commonly includes a rainbow shaded "A" to represent straight support for the Gay Pride/Equal Marriage movement.
