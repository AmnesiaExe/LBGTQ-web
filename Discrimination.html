What is Discrimination?

Discrimination is the prejudicial treatment of people and groups/communities based on characteristics such as sexual orientations, gender, or race. There are many factors that influence discrimination. Research shows that the people who discriminate are influenced by their, culture, sociological trends, community, and family beliefs. Culture influences how and whether you consider feelings. Social influence involves intentional and unintentional efforts to change a person's beliefs and behavior. Family influences include coercive interactive patterns in which a parent influences their child’s behaviour or beliefs towards something.


What are the different types of discrimination?

The people who are being discriminated against are people with a different sexual orientation other than straight. There are 4 types of discrimination that can be used: direct discrimination, indirect discrimination, harassment, victimization. Direct discrimination is when someone treats you worse/differently compared to others because of your sexuality. Indirect discrimination occurs when an organisation has a particular policy that applies to everyone but puts people with a different sexual orientation at a disadvantage. Harassment is when someone makes you feel offended, humiliated, or degraded. Victimisation is where you are treated badly because you made a complaint or sexuality related discrimination under the equality act. It can also occur to someone if they are supporting someone who has made a complaint.


How do you overcome discrimination?

Overcome discrimination: Ignore it, it’s easy to feel hurt when treated differently however you can try not to dwell on it too much and continue on with your day. Think of your strengths, being discriminated against can decrease your confidence but if you focus on your strengths instead you will feel better. Stand up for yourself, let others know that their words have affected you and make people aware that a problem exists. You can also do some practices to help calm you down such as meditation or something that will build up your mental toughness.


Who is affected by discrimination?

Discrimination affects people’s well-being and their mental state. It can make then feel worthless and vulnerable. Persistent exposure to discrimination can lead to individuals or communities to manifest in sham or end up having low self-esteem. At worst it could even result in suicide or self-harm. Discrimination can be harmful even if you aren’t targeted directly. It can be stressful if a member of your group of often being discriminated against even if you aren’t the target. The anticipation of the fact that you might be discriminated can put you in a stressful situation. People might even avoid situations where they expect they could be treated badly resulting in missed opportunities such as jobs. Overall discrimination is harmful towards people who are being target or people who associate with the target.
